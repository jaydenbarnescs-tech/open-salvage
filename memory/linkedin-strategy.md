## What's Working on LinkedIn in 2026 (AI/B2B/Japan niche)

### Hook Formats (use one of these for the first 1-2 lines)
- **The confession**: "I spent 3 months doing X the wrong way."
- **The number**: "We got 12 meetings in 30 days with zero ad spend."
- **The counterintuitive**: "Most people think [X]. They're wrong."
- **The moment**: "A client said something to me last week that changed how I think about [topic]."
- **The question**: "What does it actually take to sell AI to Japanese companies?"

### Format Rules
- **Length**: 150-250 words sweet spot (not too short to seem lazy, not a wall of text)
- **Line breaks**: Every 1-3 sentences. White space is engagement.
- **No bullet points** in the main hook — save them for the middle if needed
- **Ending**: Always close with a question or a strong statement (not "let me know your thoughts")
- **No hashtags** beyond 2-3 (they look desperate)

### Content Pillars for Jayden
1. **AI in the real world** — not hype, but actual workflows, tools, results
2. **Japan / cross-cultural business** — unique insights from working in Japan as a foreigner
3. **B2B sales stories** — real client wins, losses, lessons (anonymized)
4. **Building MGC** — behind-the-scenes of an AI-native trading company
5. **Founder observations** — things Jayden notices that others miss

### Voice & Tone
- First person, direct
- Specific details over vague claims (name the tool, the industry, the number)
- Humble about what didn't work, confident about what did
- Authentic — no corporate speak, no buzzword salad

### What to Avoid
- Starting with "I" (LinkedIn's algorithm reportedly deprioritizes this)
- Generic takes ("AI is changing everything")
- Asking for likes/shares
- Overly polished = feels fake. A little roughness is fine.
